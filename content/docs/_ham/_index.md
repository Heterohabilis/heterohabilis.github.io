# What is a *cricetus*?

People ask me this a lot, so here's the short version.

***Cricetus*** is the Latin genus name of the [**European hamster** (*Cricetus cricetus*)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_hamster) — the OG hamster, much bigger and grumpier than the chubby pet hamsters you've seen on the Internet. They live in burrows across the steppes of Eurasia, hoard absurd amounts of grain for winter, and have a reputation for fighting back against animals many times their size.

{{< figure src="./_cricetus.jpg" width="400" caption="A real *Cricetus cricetus*. Not me, but spiritually adjacent. *Courtesy of* [*Wikipedia*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_hamster#/media/File:European_hamster_(Cricetus_cricetus)_Meidling_2.jpg)." >}}

The Latin word itself probably traces back to a much older Slavic root meaning *"to crouch"* or *"to gnaw"* — fitting for an animal whose entire lifestyle is *crouch in burrow → gnaw on grain → repeat*.

---

# So why "Cybercricetus"?

Because I do roughly the same thing, except the burrow is a Linux terminal and the grain is GPU hours. **Cyber** + **cricetus** = a hamster that hoards code instead of seeds.

# How do you pronounce it?

This trips people up, so here's the breakdown:

| Form | IPA | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|
| Latin **cricetus** (classical) | /kriˈkeː.tus/ | rolled *r*, long *e*, hard *c* throughout |
| Latin **cricetus** (ecclesiastical) | /kriˈtʃeː.tus/ | the *c* before *e* softens to *ch* |
| English **cricetus** | /krɪˈsiːtəs/ | the *c* before *e* softens to *s*, schwa at the end |
| **Cybercricetus** (English) | /ˈsaɪ.bɚ.krɪˌsiː.təs/ | "SY-ber-cri-SEE-tus" |

If IPA looks like cuneiform to you, just say **"sigh-ber-cri-SEE-tus"** and you're 95% there.

And if even *that* is a mouthful — **just call me Terry**. 🐹

# Bonus: my actual name

My real name is **穆星宇 / Xingyu Mu**, which is fun in its own way:

- **Xingyu** /ɕiŋ˥y˨˩˦/ — *xīng* (star ⭐) + *yǔ* (universe 🌌). Yes, "Star Universe." My parents were ambitious.
- The **x** in Mandarin pinyin is **/ɕ/**, an alveolo-palatal fricative — somewhere between English *sh* and *s*. It does not exist in English.
- The **yu** is **/y/**, a close front rounded vowel — say *ee* with rounded lips, like the German *ü* or French *u*.

If you believe `x` /ɕ/ and `yu` /y/ are hard to pronounce — you are not alone. This is exactly why **Terry** exists as a fallback.
